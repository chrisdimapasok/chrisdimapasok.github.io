Fast Flexible Space Filling Designs for Modeling and Simulation

Modeling and simulation (M&S) is a critical component of testing and evaluating major systems in the Department of Defense. When designing a test that involves M&S, testers can use space-filling designs (SFDs) to better spread out points across the operational space. Fast flexible space-filling designs (FFSFDs) are a type of SFD useful for M&S because they work well in design spaces with disallowed combinations (i.e., nonrectangular design spaces) and permit the inclusion of categorical factors. An example of the use of an FFSFD is the planning of M&S for missile testing. Here we developed a function to create FFSFDs using clustering methods. This function can accomodate a variety of users' inputs, such as categorical factors. We also built a R-Shiny Prototype GUI app for easy use.

The algorithm works as follows.
Step 1: Generate random points in design space
Step 2: Group points into primary clusters
Step 3: Obtain center point of each cluster (centroid)
Step 4: Divide each primary cluster into subclusters
Step 5: Choose a point for each subcluster
Step 6: Randomly assign categorical labels to each subcluster
Step 7: Use an optimization criterion to generate final design
